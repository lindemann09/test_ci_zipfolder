    Field describes a study on the growing interest in cosmetic surgery treatments. He collected quality of life data from a sample of respondents, some of whom had undergone cosmetic surgery and some of whom were still on the waiting list for it (variable: surgery: 0 = waiting list, 1 = cosmetic surgery). He measured the quality of life at the time of application for the cosmetic surgery, the ‘baseline’ quality of life (variable: Base_Qol, scale 0-1, a higher score means a higher quality of life) and after some time, after the surgery had taken place for those who underwent the surgery directly, but also at the same time for the group who were still on the waiting list (variable: Post_Qol, scale 0-100, a higher score means a higher quality of life).

    Below are the results of a regression analysis with the post-measurement of quality of life as the dependent variable (Post_Qol), and in which having or not having had the cosmetic procedure (Surgery) and the ‘baseline’ quality of life (Base_Qol) are included in the model. In addition, in Model 2, the interaction between surgery and baseline quality of life (Base_Qol_surgery) was added to the model.

    In regression model 1, the t-value of Base_Qol is omitted. What is the t-value? Give a number with one decimal
