    Question

    A psychologist runs a regression with empathy as a dependent variable (Y) and gender as the independent variable (X). She finds an F-statistic of 83.1, with p < 0.001 (see the ANOVA table below). What does this mean?


    1. FALSE: The association between gender and empathy is positive and statistically significant.
    2. TRUE: The regression model with gender does significantly better in explaining the variance in empathy than a model without explanatory variables.
    3. FALSE: The mean of empathy differs significantly from the mean of gender.
    4. FALSE: The Regression Sum of Squares is positive and statistically significant.

    Solution

    The F-statistic is a measure of predictive power, and depends on the mean square for regression and the mean square error. The F-statistic is significant if the model does better in explaining the variance of the dependent variable than a model without any explanatory variables.


    1. False
    2. True
    3. False
    4. False
